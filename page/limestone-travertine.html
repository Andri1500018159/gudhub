LIMESTONE/TRAVERTINE

Limestone is an organic rock formed from the remains of tiny shells and microskeletons deposited on the sea bed. Limestone erosion is responsible for the formation of some of the world's most spectacular caverns. Travertine is a natural stone material from the limestone family. It is made of calcium carbonate and is usually found in the form of deposits near warm or hot springs.

Limestone and travertine are longlasting materials and stand up well to exposure. They are resistant to heat, scratching and staining but are vulnerable to alteration by exposure to mild acids. Certain limestones are hard enough to maintain a polish but many more can only be honed. Travertine in its way if left unfilled, may not be the most practical choice for horizontal surfaces.


Recommended use


Limestone and travertine are both commonly used for kitchen, bathroom wall tile, bathroom floor tile. Limestone also is frequently seen in countertops or can be used for fireplace hearths, mantels and fireplaces.These materials create a new stylish look to any home.

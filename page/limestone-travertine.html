LIMESTONE/TRAVERTINE

Limestone is a sedimentary rock composed mostly of calcite and aragonite minerals. It is formed at the bottom of lakes and seas. Skeletal fragments of marine organisms can be seen in limestone. Travertine is a calcium carbonate rock from the limestone family. Its natural pits and holes are made by bubbles in the hot springs where it was formed.

Limestone and travertine are both calcareous stones which are highly reactive to acidic substances such as vinegar, lemon and wine. Those acids will etch the stone by leaving marks and rings. Limestone and travertine are usually more porous than marble. The porosity will vary depending on the type of stone. The surface of both of those stones can easily be scratched.


Recommended use


Limestones and travertines are suitable for both interior and exterior projects. Most common applications include wall elevations, floors, furniture surfaces, decorative stonework. Limestone is also a popular material for exterior pavers, cladding, monuments and statues.
